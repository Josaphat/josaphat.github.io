First Post

2015-08-23

This is my first(ish) blog post. I’ve had other blogs in the past (hosted on blogger), but they’ve never stuck because their focus has always been wrong. At one point I did a blog that was a catalog of things I thought were interesting in my life. I did one with a political focus; that lasted for a couple of months. One was about song writing. As I write this, I still have a first blog post about how I was going to share my journey through designing a game and it has over 200 views (sorry to whoever saw that, became excited, and never got a second post). The blog never went further than the first post and the game itself was abandoned soon after, as school and life demanded all of my attention—or perhaps “sapped all of my motivation.”

Now I realize that I love to share things that I love. It seems obvious. Certainly, it’s much easier to stay motivated on a project when you’re doing something you love. Having an audience for your work is a very intersting way to get motivation. It forces a kind of responsibility to continue making progress and to continue sharing that progress.

I’ve got a few projects rattling around in my head. I’ll publish those in case you, my dear reader, decide that my proposals are great ideas you should spend your time on. Until I get the ball rolling on one of those projects I’m going to dedicate this space to writing little blurbs about programming, my experiences, etc.

I haven’t said what this blog is going to be about. It’s going to be about everything, with the common thread being that it’s relevant to me somehow. If that sounds egotistical, it is; but writing a blog is quite egotistical to begin with. I dare to believe that the things I have to say are worth sharing with the world.

Might as well.

My hope is that you’ll find what I have to say useful. If not, then I’ll settle for interesting or entertaining.
